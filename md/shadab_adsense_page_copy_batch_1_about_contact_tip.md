# About Shadab Chow

ShadabChow.com is an independent publishing platform focused on breaking news, crime reporting, corruption, public accountability, exclusive footage, developing stories, and commentary.

The site is led by Shadab Chow and built around a simple purpose: publish information that helps people understand what happened, why it matters, and what still needs to be verified.

This platform is not a traditional newsroom, political campaign, gossip page, or ecommerce store. It is a personal-brand publishing site for independent reporting, public-interest coverage, and documented stories.

## What this site covers

ShadabChow.com focuses on coverage areas where facts, context, evidence, and accountability matter:

- Breaking news
- Crime and public safety
- Corruption and misuse of power
- Public accountability
- Exclusive or submitted footage
- Developing stories
- Court, law enforcement, and public-record-related reporting
- Commentary on public-interest issues

Some stories may involve allegations, arrests, lawsuits, violence, sensitive footage, or graphic details. When coverage includes disturbing or sensitive material, the site may use viewer-discretion warnings, context notes, source notes, and update labels.

## Editorial purpose

The goal is to make public-interest information easier to find, understand, and review.

ShadabChow.com aims to:

- Separate confirmed facts from claims or allegations
- Identify what is known and what remains unclear
- Add context to viral or developing material
- Correct or update stories when new information becomes available
- Avoid presenting allegations as proven facts
- Provide a clear way for readers to request corrections or submit relevant information

The site may publish original reporting, commentary, summaries of public information, embedded media, submitted tips, public records, source material, or curated context around developing stories. Content is reviewed before publication, but developing stories may change as more information becomes available.

## Independence and transparency

ShadabChow.com is independently operated. Editorial coverage is not sold to advertisers, sponsors, donors, or outside organizations.

The site may use advertising, sponsorship, affiliate links, donations, newsletters, or reader-support tools to support operations. Any sponsored content or paid partnership should be clearly labeled when it appears.

Advertising support does not give advertisers control over editorial coverage.

## Corrections and updates

Accuracy matters. If a story contains an error, missing context, outdated information, or unclear wording, readers can request a correction through the Corrections Policy page or Contact page.

When a correction or material update is made, the site may update the article, add an editor’s note, revise the headline or description, or clarify what changed.

## Submit information

Readers, witnesses, sources, and community members may submit tips, documents, photos, videos, or public-interest leads through the Submit a Tip page.

Submitting information does not guarantee publication, response, confidentiality, payment, or legal protection. Do not submit illegal material, hacked material, stolen private records, or anything you do not have the right to share.

## Contact

For questions, corrections, press inquiries, story leads, advertising inquiries, or general contact, use the Contact page.

For story leads, use Submit a Tip when possible.

- Advertising or sponsorship inquiries
- Media or collaboration inquiries
- Website issues
- Privacy-related questions

## Corrections and editorial concerns

If you believe an article contains an error, missing context, outdated information, or a misleading statement, please include:

- The article title or URL
- The specific sentence, headline, image, or claim at issue
- What you believe is inaccurate or incomplete
- Any supporting source, record, document, or public reference
- Your contact information, if you want a response

Correction requests are reviewed based on the available evidence, public records, sourcing, and editorial judgment.

Sending a correction request does not guarantee that a story will be removed, rewritten, or changed, but good-faith correction requests are taken seriously.

## Story tips and sensitive information

If you have a story tip, use the Submit a Tip page.

Do not submit illegal material, hacked material, stolen private records, passwords, private medical information, Social Security numbers, financial account numbers, or other sensitive personal data unless there is a clear legal right and public-interest basis to share it.

If your information is sensitive, consider whether ordinary email or website forms are appropriate for your situation. Do not put yourself or others at unnecessary legal, personal, or physical risk.

## Advertising and sponsorship inquiries

ShadabChow.com may consider advertising, sponsorships, or brand partnerships that fit the site’s audience and standards.

Advertising or sponsorship does not purchase editorial coverage. Sponsored content, if accepted, should be clearly labeled.

Do not use this page to request that ads be clicked, viewed, manipulated, or artificially boosted.

## Response times

Messages may not receive an immediate response. Some messages may not receive a response at all, especially if they are incomplete, abusive, spammy, promotional, or unrelated to the site’s coverage.

## Page copy

# Submit a Tip

Have information tied to a public-interest story?

Use this page to submit a tip, lead, document, image, video, public record, witness account, or other information that may help with reporting.

ShadabChow.com reviews tips related to breaking news, crime, corruption, public accountability, exclusive footage, developing stories, and commentary.

## Before you submit

Please read these guidelines carefully.

Submitting a tip does not guarantee:

- Publication
- A response
- Confidentiality
- Payment
- Legal protection
- Removal or publication of any article
- That your information will be used

Tips may be reviewed, verified, rejected, ignored, archived, or used as background for future reporting.

## What you can submit

Useful tips may include:

- Firsthand information about a public-interest event
- Photos or videos you have the right to share
- Links to public records or official documents
- Court records, police reports, meeting records, or public filings
- Information about corruption, misconduct, abuse of power, fraud, or public safety
- Context about a developing story
- Corrections or updates to published coverage
- Leads involving crime, public accountability, or community impact

Include as much context as possible:

- What happened?
- Where did it happen?
- When did it happen?
- Who was involved?
- How do you know?
- Is there documentation?
- Is anyone else able to verify it?
- Is the information public, private, alleged, or confirmed?

## What not to submit

Do not submit:

- Illegal material
- Hacked material
- Stolen private records
- Passwords or login credentials
- Social Security numbers
- Bank account numbers
- Private medical records
- Revenge content or non-consensual intimate material
- Material involving minors in a sexual or exploitative context
- Threats, harassment, blackmail, or extortion attempts
- Content you do not have the right to share

If a matter involves immediate danger, call emergency services or contact the appropriate authorities first.

## Sensitive footage and graphic content

Some public-interest stories involve violence, crime scenes, injury, nudity, disturbing content, or other sensitive material.

If you submit sensitive footage, clearly explain:

- What the footage shows
- When and where it was recorded
- Who recorded it
- Whether you have permission to share it
- Whether minors are present
- Whether anyone is injured, deceased, exposed, or identifiable
- Whether the footage has been edited
- Whether it has already been posted publicly

ShadabChow.com may decline to publish graphic, exploitative, misleading, or unverifiable material.

When sensitive content is published, the site may use viewer-discretion warnings, context labels, source notes, and editorial framing.

## Allegations and legal status

Tips involving accusations, arrests, lawsuits, investigations, or misconduct are treated carefully.

An allegation is not proof. An arrest is not a conviction. People named in a story are presumed innocent unless proven otherwise through the legal process.

If you submit allegations, include supporting records or explain how the information can be verified.

## Anonymous tips

You may submit information without including your name, but anonymous tips can be harder to verify.

If you want follow-up, provide a safe way to contact you.

Do not assume that ordinary web forms, email, messaging apps, or social media are fully secure or anonymous. If your situation involves legal danger, retaliation risk, workplace risk, immigration risk, or personal safety concerns, consider speaking with a qualified attorney or trusted advisor before sending sensitive material.

## Verification and editorial review

Tips are reviewed based on:

- Newsworthiness
- Public-interest value
- Source reliability
- Supporting documentation
- Verification potential
- Legal and safety concerns
- Editorial standards
- Potential harm to private individuals

The site may contact you for more information, but it is not required to do so.

## No payment for tips unless explicitly agreed

Do not send a tip expecting payment.

ShadabChow.com does not promise payment, rewards, compensation, or any benefit in exchange for tips, documents, images, videos, or ad interactions.

## Ad and monetization note

Do not click, view, refresh, or interact with ads to support a tip, story, donation, or request.

Advertising, if present on the site, must remain separate from editorial decisions, tip review, and publication decisions.

## Submit your information

When sending a tip, include:

- Your name, if you want to provide it
- Your email or phone, if you want a response
- The story lead or information
- Links to documents, photos, videos, or public records
- Whether the information is public, private, alleged, confirmed, or developing
- Any safety concerns
