# Copyright / DMCA Policy

Effective date: [Insert date]

ShadabChow.com respects intellectual property rights and expects readers, contributors, sources, advertisers, and other users to do the same.

This Copyright / DMCA Policy explains how copyright owners can submit copyright complaints, how the site may review takedown requests, how submitted media is handled, and how counter-notices may be processed where applicable.

This page is intended to provide a clear copyright review process for a publisher site that may contain original reporting, commentary, public-interest coverage, submitted footage, images, videos, embedded media, documents, public records, screenshots, quotes, excerpts, and links.

## Copyright ownership

Unless otherwise stated, original content created for ShadabChow.com is owned by ShadabChow.com or its respective author, publisher, creator, licensor, or rights holder.

This may include:

- Original articles
- Headlines
- Page copy
- Editorial notes
- Original graphics
- Original layout elements
- Original photos or videos
- Compilations
- Design elements
- Original commentary
- Original summaries and analysis

Third-party content remains the property of its respective owners.

## Public-interest reporting and fair use

ShadabChow.com may publish, quote, embed, reference, summarize, comment on, or link to third-party material for purposes such as news reporting, criticism, commentary, public accountability, public-interest analysis, education, or context.

Depending on the circumstances, some uses may be protected by fair use, fair dealing, license, permission, public-domain status, public-record status, platform embedding rules, or other legal principles.

Examples may include:

- Quoting a short excerpt for commentary
- Embedding a public social media post
- Referencing a public document
- Showing a limited screenshot for news context
- Discussing a public statement
- Reporting on a video or image that is itself newsworthy
- Using a thumbnail or cropped image to identify a public-interest story

A copyright complaint does not automatically mean content will be removed. The site may review the claim, context, public-interest value, source, usage, amount used, and applicable legal considerations.

## Submitted photos, videos, and documents

Readers, sources, witnesses, and community members may submit photos, videos, documents, screenshots, links, or other materials.

By submitting material, you represent that:

- You have the right to submit it
- The material does not violate copyright law
- The material does not violate another person’s rights
- The material was not obtained unlawfully
- The material does not include illegal or prohibited content
- The material may be reviewed for possible editorial use

Submitting material does not guarantee publication, payment, credit, confidentiality, legal protection, or a response.

Do not submit copyrighted material that you do not own or do not have permission or legal basis to share.

## Embedded third-party content

Articles may include embedded content from third-party platforms, such as videos, social media posts, documents, maps, or other media.

Embedded content may be hosted by the third-party platform, not by ShadabChow.com.

If you believe embedded content infringes your rights, you may contact the platform hosting the material in addition to contacting ShadabChow.com.

The site may remove or replace embedded material when appropriate, but it may not control the underlying third-party platform.

## Copyright complaint process

If you believe material on ShadabChow.com infringes your copyright, submit a copyright complaint with enough information for review.

Your complaint should include:

1. Your full legal name
2. Your organization, if applicable
3. Your email address
4. Your phone number, if available
5. Identification of the copyrighted work you claim was infringed
6. The URL of the allegedly infringing material on ShadabChow.com
7. A description of where the allegedly infringing material appears on the page
8. A statement explaining your ownership or authorization to act for the copyright owner
9. A statement explaining why you believe the use is unauthorized
10. Any relevant registration number, source URL, publication date, or proof of ownership
11. A statement that the information in your complaint is accurate
12. Your physical or electronic signature

Incomplete complaints may be difficult to review.

## Suggested complaint statement

You may include this statement in your complaint:

```text
I have a good-faith belief that the use of the copyrighted material identified in this notice is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law. I state that the information in this notice is accurate, and that I am the copyright owner or authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner.
```

## Where to send a copyright complaint

Use the Contact page unless a dedicated copyright email is later provided.

- Contact page: `/pages/contact`

When submitting a copyright complaint, include “Copyright Complaint” or “DMCA Notice” in the subject or first line of your message.

## Review of copyright complaints

After receiving a copyright complaint, ShadabChow.com may:

- Review the material
- Request more information
- Remove the material
- Disable access to the material
- Replace the material
- Add attribution or context
- Determine the use is permitted
- Decline an incomplete or unsupported complaint
- Forward the complaint to a contributor, source, host, service provider, or legal advisor
- Preserve records related to the dispute

The site may act quickly in some cases and may take more time in others, depending on complexity, urgency, legal risk, and available evidence.

## Counter-notices

If material you submitted or own was removed because of a copyright complaint and you believe the removal was mistaken, you may submit a counter-notice where applicable.

A counter-notice should include:

1. Your full legal name
2. Your contact information
3. Identification of the removed material
4. The URL where the material appeared before removal, if available
5. A statement explaining why you believe the material was removed by mistake or misidentification
6. A statement that you consent to the jurisdiction required by applicable law, if applicable
7. A statement that the information in your counter-notice is accurate
8. Your physical or electronic signature

Do not submit a counter-notice unless you understand the legal consequences. Consider speaking with an attorney before submitting one.

## Repeat infringement

ShadabChow.com may restrict, block, remove, or decline submissions from users, contributors, sources, advertisers, or accounts that repeatedly submit infringing material or abusive copyright claims.

The site may also remove repeat infringer access where appropriate.

## False or abusive claims

Do not submit false, misleading, abusive, or bad-faith copyright complaints.

Misusing the copyright complaint process can harm lawful reporting, commentary, criticism, public accountability, and fair use.

False claims may result in rejection of the request, publication of clarifying information, legal exposure, or restriction from future submissions.

## News reporting and takedown requests

A request to remove content because it is embarrassing, critical, negative, newsworthy, controversial, or unfavorable is not the same as a copyright complaint.

For factual errors, privacy concerns, outdated information, or public-interest takedown requests, use the Corrections Policy or Contact page.

Copyright complaints should identify specific copyrighted material and explain the claimed infringement.

## Attribution requests

If you believe material should include additional attribution, contact the site with:

- The article URL
- The relevant image, video, quote, document, or source
- The requested attribution
- Supporting proof or source link

The site may add, update, or decline attribution based on editorial judgment, legal considerations, source reliability, and available evidence.

## Use of ShadabChow.com content

You may link to public pages on ShadabChow.com.

You may quote short excerpts for commentary, criticism, news discussion, or fair-use purposes where permitted by law, with appropriate attribution and a link when possible.

You may not copy, scrape, mirror, sell, republish, or redistribute substantial portions of the site without permission, except where allowed by law.

## Advertising and copyright

Advertising, sponsorship, donations, or business relationships do not control copyright decisions.

Advertisers, sponsors, donors, vendors, and partners do not receive the right to force removal of public-interest reporting through copyright claims.

Do not click, view, refresh, or interact with ads as part of a copyright complaint, copyright request, takedown request, or ownership dispute.

## Privacy and personal information in complaints

Copyright complaints may include personal information such as name, organization, email address, phone number, signature, and claim details.

This information may be used to review the claim, communicate with parties, preserve records, respond to legal requirements, contact service providers, or process disputes.

For more information, review the Privacy Policy.

## Changes to this policy

This Copyright / DMCA Policy may be updated from time to time.

When updates are made, the effective date may change. Continued use of the site after changes means you accept the updated policy.

## Contact

For copyright complaints or questions, use the Contact page.
